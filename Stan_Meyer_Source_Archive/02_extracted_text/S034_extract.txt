Vacuum 
 
 
It is fairly well known in the field that applying a vacuum to an electrolysis cell 
will cause more gas production for less energy. This is because the vacuum being 
pulled on the water itself is trying to pull the water molecules apart.  
 
If we apply a strong enough vacuum to a water chamber, we can cause the water 
to boil at room temperature. The more pressure there is on the water, the more 
energy is required to split or boil the water and the less pressure there is, the less 
energy is required. 
 
Many experimenters apply their engine's vacuum directly to their electrolyzers 
to increase gas production for less input but it is also an obvious direct route to 
suck the water fuel right into the intake manifold.

needed.
